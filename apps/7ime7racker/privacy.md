# Privacy Policy for 7ime7racker

Effective date: May 10, 2026

This privacy policy explains how 7ime7racker handles data in the iOS, iPadOS, watchOS, widget, shortcut, and Live Activity parts of the app. It is written both for App Store review and for people who find the source code or project files in a public GitHub repository.

## Short Version

7ime7racker is designed as a local-first time tracker.

- The app does not require an account with Flatbrainer.
- The app does not contain advertising SDKs.
- The app does not sell personal data.
- The app does not use third-party analytics or third-party crash reporting SDKs.
- Your time entries, projects, notes, rates, clients, areas, and location history are stored on your device unless you choose to export them or enable Apple iCloud sync.
- Some optional features use Apple system services, such as Location Services, Calendar access, iCloud, App Store purchases, Maps/geocoding, notifications, widgets, Live Activities, Shortcuts, and Watch support.

## Data You May Store in the App

Depending on how you use 7ime7racker, the app may store the following data on your device:

- Project names, colors, tags, client names, hourly rates, budgets, invoice status, and calendar matching keywords.
- Time entries, start and end times, durations, notes, and whether entries are billable.
- Location coordinates attached to time entries, if location-based tracking is used.
- Areas you create for location automation, including area names, coordinates, radius, address labels, notes, and assigned project automation.
- Area stay history, such as when you entered or left a saved area.
- Calendar event details you choose to import or use to draft time entries.
- App settings, purchase state, daily review dismissals, notification preferences, widget snapshots, Live Activity state, shortcut state, and Watch snapshots.
- Exported files that you explicitly create, such as CSV, backup, or invoice-related files.

This data is used to provide time tracking, location automation, daily review, reports, exports, widgets, shortcuts, Live Activities, and Watch features.

## Local Storage

By default, app data is stored locally on your device. Flatbrainer does not operate a server that receives your time tracking database.

Deleting entries, projects, areas, notes, or exports inside the app removes those items from the app's local data store, subject to normal device backup and operating system behavior.

Deleting the app removes its local app data from the device. If you have enabled iCloud sync, synced data may also exist in iCloud until it is deleted through the app or through Apple's iCloud data management tools.

## Optional iCloud Sync

If you enable iCloud sync, 7ime7racker uses Apple's CloudKit/iCloud infrastructure to sync your app data through your Apple ID. Synced data is stored in your private iCloud account, not on a Flatbrainer-operated server.

iCloud sync is optional. If you do not enable it, the app stores its database locally on the device.

Apple's handling of iCloud data is governed by Apple's privacy policies and your Apple ID/iCloud settings.

## Location Data

7ime7racker can use location permission for current-location display, map centering, saved areas, background area monitoring, automatic or suggested project timers, and address-based location notifications.

When location features are enabled, the app may store:

- Coordinates for saved areas.
- Coordinates for entry start and end points.
- Area radius and area labels.
- Enter/leave times for saved areas.
- Resolved address labels for coordinates, when available.

Location permission is controlled by iOS Settings. You can disable location access at any time in Settings. If you disable location access, location automation and background area tracking will not work.

The app may use Apple system services such as Maps, Location Services, and geocoding to display maps or convert coordinates into address labels. Those Apple services are governed by Apple's privacy policies and device settings.

## Calendar Data

Calendar access is optional. If you grant calendar permission, 7ime7racker can read calendar events so you can create or suggest draft time entries from events you choose to use.

Calendar event data is used for time tracking features inside the app. The app does not send your calendar events to a Flatbrainer server.

You can revoke calendar access at any time in iOS Settings.

## Purchases

In-app purchases are handled by Apple's StoreKit and App Store systems. 7ime7racker uses purchase information to unlock purchased app features.

Flatbrainer does not receive your full payment card details. Apple may provide purchase status or transaction information needed to operate App Store purchases.

## Notifications, Widgets, Live Activities, Shortcuts, and Watch

The app may use local notifications to remind you about timers, daily review, or area events. Notification content is generated by the app on your device.

Widgets, Live Activities, Shortcuts, and Watch support may use small local snapshots of your time tracking state, such as active timer information, today's totals, or review status. These snapshots are stored locally in the app group shared between the app and its extensions.

## Exports, Backups, and Sharing

If you export, back up, email, share, or save files from 7ime7racker, you choose where those files go. Exported files may include time entries, dates, notes, project details, billable information, client names, location coordinates, area names, or other app data depending on the export type.

Once you share an export with another app or service, that other app or service handles the file under its own privacy policy.

## Data Not Collected by Flatbrainer

The app is not designed to collect the following data for Flatbrainer:

- Advertising identifiers.
- Third-party tracking identifiers.
- Browsing history.
- Contacts.
- Photos.
- Health data.
- Microphone or camera data.
- Payment card numbers.
- Analytics events sent to a Flatbrainer server.
- Crash reports sent to a third-party crash reporting service by the app.

Apple may still provide developers with App Store Connect information such as app downloads, sales, subscription or purchase information, crash diagnostics, and aggregated usage metrics according to Apple's developer tools and user privacy settings.

## Public GitHub Repository

This policy describes the 7ime7racker app. If you interact with a public GitHub repository for the app, GitHub may process your GitHub account information, issues, pull requests, comments, and repository activity under GitHub's own privacy policies.

Do not post private time entries, client names, invoices, addresses, coordinates, screenshots, backups, or other personal data in public GitHub issues or pull requests.

## Data Retention and Deletion

Data you create in the app remains in the app until you delete it, export it, overwrite it, disable/delete synced copies, or uninstall the app.

To remove app data:

1. Delete entries, projects, areas, and notes in the app where available.
2. Delete exported files wherever you saved or shared them.
3. If iCloud sync is enabled, remove synced app data through the app and/or your Apple ID iCloud storage controls.
4. Delete the app from your device to remove local app data.

## Children

7ime7racker is a productivity tool for time tracking. It is not directed to children and is not intended to knowingly collect personal data from children.

## Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated when the app's features or data handling change. The effective date at the top of the policy will be updated when meaningful changes are made.

## Contact

For privacy questions, use the developer contact information listed for 7ime7racker on the App Store or the contact method published by the repository owner.

If you use GitHub to ask a general question, do not include personal data or private screenshots in a public issue or pull request.
